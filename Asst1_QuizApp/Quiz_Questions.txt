What is “Pau Hana”?
A) A type of fish
B) The end of the workday
C) A Hawaiian chant
D) A luau dish
B
"Pau hana," which means "end of work," is usually used for the time after work that is spent socializing or relaxing.

What is the Hawaiian word for “responsibility” or “privilege”?
A) Kuleana
B) Mana
C) Pono
D) Aina
A
"Kuleana" means privilege or responsibility which shows that rights come with responsibilities
---
What is the term for a native Hawaiian story or legend? 
A) Mele
B) Moʻolelo
C) Hula
D) Kahuna
B
Moʻolelo represents Hawaiian storytelling, traditions, and history, regularly passed down through generations to keep alive culture and heritage.
---
What is a “Kahuna”?
A) Leader of a hula group
B) A Hawaiian shaman or priest
C) A type of canoe
D) A Hawaiian mountain
B
A honored Hawaiian priest or shaman known as a "kahuna" is usually seen as a healer or keeper of spiritual wisdom.
---
What does “E komo mai” mean?
A) Goodbye
B) Welcome
C) Come back soon
D) Blessings
B
The greeting "e komo mai," which means "welcome," can be used to send an invitation to someone into a place or gathering.
